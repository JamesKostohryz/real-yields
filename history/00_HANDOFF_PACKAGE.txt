====================================================================
1. MODULE & PURPOSE
====================================================================
Reconstructed a monthly U.S. Treasury REAL yield curve at 1/5/10/20/30-year tenors, Sept
1877–June 2026, in two risk concepts (ex-ante = nominal − expected inflation; TIPS-like =
nominal − breakeven), plus the underlying long-horizon expected-inflation term structure. It
extends the Cleveland Fed real-rate series (starts 1982) back to the gold standard, and feeds
the AEG/cost-of-equity model's real (TIPS-based) risk-free input.

9. ANYTHING I'D REGRET LOSING (decisions & why)
====================================================================
- NO-BLEND SPLICE at 1982: replaced an earlier 1982-86 blend (which masked the disinflation E[π]
  overshoot) with a continuity correction that fixes the overshoot at the join, decaying to 0 by
  1976 (+0.29pp organic step, pre-1976 untouched).
- CLEVELAND VALIDATION: our independent ex-ante ≈ Cleveland real rate, corr 0.94 / gap 0.08pp.
  Cleveland real rate ≈ EX-ANTE (not TIPS-like).
- TIPS VALIDATION: Cleveland-anchored real vs ACTUAL TIPS corr 0.93-0.99, MAE 0.14-0.46pp
  (1999-2026); adopting market phi makes TIPS-like = actual TIPS exactly.
- Cleveland publishes real rate only at 1mo/1y/10y but E[π] at ALL 1-30; nominal − Cleveland E[π]
  reproduces their 10y real to 0.37pp → that's how every tenor is Cleveland-anchored.
- His production "real" is TIPS-anchored (feds200805) = TIPS-like; his "phi" = market IRP; final
  TIPS-like should tie to his phi where TIPS exist.
- Shiller CPI (Warren-Pearson wholesale pre-1913) NOT used for CPI (GFD better); Shiller used only
  for Homer-Sylla nominal cross-check. Groen bottoms at 1971-09, ends 2013-04.
- Carried from earlier chats: real-terms operation; CPI-U now, BEA nonresidential deflator flagged
